## üì∏ PHOTOGRAPHY GUIDELINES

### STYLE & TONE

**Overall:** Lifestyle photography celebrating transformation and health
**Mood:** Inspirational, achievable, real people (not overly staged)
**Quality:** Professional, high-resolution (min 1080px width for Instagram)

### IMAGERY CATEGORIES

**1. TRANSFORMATION IMAGERY**
- Before/After style photos (but lifestyle-focused, not medical)
- Person looking energized, healthy, confident
- Natural lighting preferred
- People in normal life contexts (work, family, exercise)

**2. LIFESTYLE IMAGERY**
- People eating healthy food (green vegetables, whole foods)
- Active lifestyle (exercise, outdoor activities, sports)
- Morning routines (fresh, energized)
- Family/social moments (connection, health as lifestyle)

**3. SCIENCE/AUTHORITY IMAGERY**
- Lab settings, microscopes, research visuals
- Medical/health authority (doctors, health professionals)
- Charts and data visualization
- Keep scientific but accessible

**4. PRODUCT IMAGERY**
- Reset Primal bottle/packaging
- Product in lifestyle context (person holding, using)
- Close-up of product with ingredients visible
- Package with guarantee badge visible

### PHOTO COLOR GRADING

- **Warm Tones:** For before photos (struggle, current state)
- **Cool to Warm Transition:** For transformation journey
- **Bright & Vibrant:** For after/success photos (harmonize with green)
- **Green Tint:** Subtle green color grading to maintain brand consistency
- **High Contrast:** Ensure text overlay readability

### DO's & DON'Ts

**‚úÖ DO:**
- Use real people with authentic expressions
- Show diversity (age, gender, ethnicity)
- Capture genuine moments (not overly posed)
- Include lifestyle context
- Use natural lighting when possible
- Show health/vitality through body language

**‚ùå DON'T:**
- Overly staged/fake looking photos
- Medical imagery that's too clinical or scary
- Before/after that's unrealistic or manipulated
- Photos with watermarks or poor quality
- Imagery that looks generic or stock-photo-like
